# Concept Ownership, Before

Phase 0 baseline record for the repository repositioning. This document records where each load-bearing concept is defined and where it is restated or heavily used, as the repository stands at branch `repositioning-exploration` (HEAD `dc7a897`). It records the present state only; decisions belong to the ADR.

Names drop the `draft-mcguinness-` prefix. Section anchors in parentheses are the kramdown-rfc anchors in the named draft.

## The repository's own ownership statements

The repository states its definitional model in three places.

1. README, "The core" entry for oauth-mission:

   > The mandatory core, the **issuance profile**. Defines the Mission, the Mission Intent and Authority Set, the approval event and its `intent_hash` / `authority_hash` integrity anchors, the `mission` token claim, the subset rule, and state-gated issuance. Every other document builds on this one.

   And the README's entry for Mission Substrate Requirements:

   > Changes nothing for the three existing bindings, which remain authoritative for themselves; the core remains the model's definitional home.

2. mission-architecture, "The Mission Substrate" ({#substrate}), consolidates the interface informationally and assigns a home per primitive:

   > This section consolidates that interface: eight primitives, each with its normative home and its consumers. Every sentence mirrors a rule the named profile states normatively.

   Each of its eight primitive subsections carries an explicit "Home:" sentence pointing at a core section (or, for freshness mechanics, at the status and runtime profiles). Its Binding Checklist ({#binding-checklist}) adds: "this section remains the informative summary, and the existing bindings remain authoritative for themselves" and "The per-profile Mission Substrate sections remain the authoritative per-consumer statements of this interface."

3. mission-substrate, "Status: The Binding Contract" ({#status}), describes both the contract's authority and the migration rule:

   > This document is the family's binding-neutral contract. A requirement stated here binds a new binding that claims conformance ({{conformance}}). The three existing bindings, the OAuth core, the Mission Authority Server, and the AAuth binding, predate this document and are its conforming instantiations: the contract was extracted from them, and {{crosswalk}} records where each satisfies it. Their own texts remain authoritative for their definitions, and where this document and the core appear to differ, the core governs.
   >
   > Ownership migrates by touch, not by relocation. When a binding-neutral definition next changes substantively, the change lands in this document and the owning binding section becomes a reference to it; no change is ever made solely to move words.

   The substrate's requirements section adds: "Each subsection states the requirement on the binding, the home of the normative definition (a section of the core), and the consuming profiles; a summary here never overrides its home." And its introduction: "It restates no definition: each requirement points at the core section that owns it."

Net: per the repository's own statements, the current normative owner of every model-level definition is **oauth-mission** (the core), except where a companion profile owns a surface it introduced (status, runtime, and the per-profile evidence objects, as itemized below). The substrate document is a normative contract for future bindings that today points back at the core for every definition, with declared ownership migration by touch.

## Observations recorded (not recommendations)

- Every concept traces its primary definition to oauth-mission or to a companion that owns the surface it introduced, with one partial exception: the substrate document states the credential-or-join either/or (and the Full/Partial provision terms) for new bindings, while pointing every underlying definition at a core section, per its own text ("It restates no definition").
- Four concepts are split-owned across two or more documents by design as the repository states it: lifecycle (core owns the state space, status owns the added states and transitions), delegation (core owns the act chain, child-delegation owns Child Missions), denial surfaces (three surfaces, each owned once, consolidated informatively by the architecture), and credential-or-join (core, substrate, and MAS each own one leg).
- Two informative documents restate nearly the whole matrix without owning any row: mission-architecture (by design, "Home:" per primitive) and mission-security-model (component-by-component consolidation).
- The ownership-migration rule in the substrate's Status section is the repository's only stated mechanism for moving a definition out of the core, and it is conditional on the next substantive change ("by touch, not by relocation").
